Iterative Development • Weekly Sprints • 2025

Agile Philosophy

Individuals and interactions over processes and tools

Working software over comprehensive documentation

Customer collaboration over contract negotiation

Responding to change over following a plan

Plans are useful, but when reality differs from the plan, the team must adapt

Sprint Structure

Time-boxed iterations with fixed duration (1-4 weeks)

Teams commit to delivering a defined set of work during each sprint

Each sprint produces a potentially shippable increment

Fixed duration forces realistic planning and capacity learning

For this course: One-week sprints for rapid feedback

Core Ceremonies

Sprint Planning: What will we deliver? How will we accomplish it?

Daily Standup: 15-minute sync - progress, plans, and blockers

Sprint Review: Demonstrate working functionality to stakeholders

Sprint Retrospective: Reflect on process and commit to improvements

Team Roles

Product Owner: Maintains prioritized backlog, decides what gets built

Scrum Master: Facilitates process, removes obstacles, keeps meetings time-boxed

Development Team: Self-organizing team that executes work and delivers increments

Cross-functional teams collaborate on shared goals rather than isolated specialties

Artifacts

Product Backlog: Prioritized list of all work that might be built

Sprint Backlog: Work committed to the current sprint

Increment: Sum of all completed work meeting the Definition of Done

For infrastructure: Done means tested, documented, secured, and deployable
